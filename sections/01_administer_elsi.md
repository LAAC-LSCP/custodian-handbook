# How to Administer ELSI

The ELSI (Early Language and Social Interaction) protocol is the core data collection instrument used across ExELang sites. Follow these steps carefully to ensure consistency across the project.

## Before the Session

1. **Schedule the appointment** — Book the session in the shared calendar at least 48 hours in advance. Include the participant ID (not their name) in the event title.
2. **Prepare the materials** — Print the ELSI stimulus booklet from the shared drive (`/Materials/ELSI/Stimuli_vX.X.pdf`). Confirm you have the correct version for the current data collection wave.
3. **Check equipment** — Test audio recording equipment at least 30 minutes before the session. Ensure battery levels are adequate and that storage media is blank.
4. **Confirm consent** — Verify that the participant's guardian has signed the informed consent form and that it is filed in the appropriate folder.

## During the Session

1. **Introduction script** — Read the standardised introduction verbatim (see `ELSI_Admin_Protocol.pdf`, p. 3). Do not deviate from the script.
2. **Recording** — Start the audio recorder before entering the room with the participant. Label the recording with the format: `SITECODE_PARTICIPANTID_YYYYMMDD`.
3. **Scoring live** — Use the paper scoring sheet during administration. Circle responses clearly. Do not make corrections with correction fluid — cross out and initial any errors.
4. **Timing** — Adhere strictly to item timing guidelines. Use a silent timer on your phone if needed.

## After the Session

1. **Transfer recordings** — Upload audio files to the secure server within 24 hours using the procedure in [How to Add New Data to the Server](#how-to-add-new-tools-on-server).
2. **Enter scores** — Enter paper scores into the REDCap database on the same day. Double-check entries before saving.
3. **File paperwork** — Scan consent forms and upload to the secure shared drive. Physical copies go in the locked cabinet.
4. **Flag any anomalies** — If anything unusual occurred during the session (interruption, participant distress, equipment failure), complete an Incident Note and email it to the PI.

> **Note:** If this is your first time administering the ELSI, you must complete a reliability training session with a certified administrator and reach 85% agreement before collecting independent data.
